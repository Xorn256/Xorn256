# Design Philosophy

## Core Principles
- Prefer composable systems over fragmented mechanics
- Design new content to integrate into existing rule ecosystems rather than bypass them
- Favor shared gameplay rules between players and NPCs where practical
- Prioritize interaction depth and systemic consistency over isolated feature design
- Build systems that scale cleanly with future content additions

## Difficulty & Scaling Philosophy
- Favor horizontal complexity before vertical stat inflation
- Increase challenge through layered mechanics, timing, positioning, resource pressure, and decision density
- Use numerical scaling as support rather than the foundation of difficulty
- Avoid unnecessary stat bloat when systemic depth can create more meaningful gameplay
- Create additional opportunities for player mistakes and adaptation instead of relying purely on larger numbers

## Applied Design Approach (Conflux)
These principles were directly implemented within the Divinity Conflux combat overhaul framework.

Conflux uses a layered difficulty structure combining:
- baseline world difficulty selection
- optional in-game challenge modifiers
- expanded enemy behavior layers
- systemic combat interactions
- scaling encounter pressure through mechanics rather than raw statistical escalation alone

The project serves as a practical implementation of long-term systems design philosophies developed across decades of online game analysis, modding, and mechanics experimentation.

## MMO Systems Discovery & Applied Mechanics Analysis

### Dark Age of Camelot – Regen Aura Timing Interaction
A timing-based interaction was identified involving Paladin regeneration aura behavior.

By toggling the aura at specific tick intervals, overlapping or duplicated regeneration ticks could occur under certain server timing conditions, significantly increasing sustain efficiency beyond intended design behavior.

The discovery was informed by prior experience with EverQuest Bard timing mechanics (“twisting”), which relied on managing overlapping buff timing windows.

The interaction later became widely adopted before eventually being normalized through later game updates.

### Dark Age of Camelot – Geometry / Boundary Interaction Discovery
Identified geometry-related movement interactions that allowed traversal into restricted or unintended world areas.

The behavior was documented at the time through in-game screenshots (later lost due to hardware failure) and discussed directly with in-game moderation staff.

This reflected early interest in world systems, spatial logic, and edge-case interaction behavior.
